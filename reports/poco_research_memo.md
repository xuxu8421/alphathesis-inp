# Power Inductors: AI's Quiet Power-Delivery Bottleneck
*As GPU power crosses 1kW, the magnetics that feed the chip become a constraint, not a commodity. POCO (Bokede New Material, 300811) is the vertical-power-delivery chip-inductor leader.*

> **As of** 2026-06-24  ·  **Generated by** AlphaThesis (an AlphaAnalyst extension)  ·  **Citation coverage** 100% (16/16 fact-claims sourced)

**One-liner.** Everyone watches the GPU and HBM; almost nobody watches the inductor that delivers 200A into the back of that GPU. As accelerators cross 1kW, ferrite wirewound inductors saturate and die, alloy molded inductors become the only option, per-board content multiplies, and the value per part rises 3-5x — and POCO, alone among peers, owns the whole stack from soft-magnetic powder to the finished VPD chip inductor and ships it straight into NVIDIA.

## Verdict
Structurally bullish on the AI power-inductor / VPD chip-inductor bottleneck, with POCO (Bokede New Material) as the cleanest pure-play. The content story is supply-architecture-driven (1kW+ accelerators force alloy molded inductors and vertical power delivery), the leader is uniquely integrated from soft-magnetic powder to finished device, and capacity — not demand — is the near-term swing factor.

**Conviction:** High (structural content growth), with risk concentrated in single-customer concentration, valuation, and capacity-ramp execution.

## Bull thesis (constructive agents)
### Demand shock
- A conventional server uses ~30-50 inductors; a 4-GPU AI server uses 80-120, and inductors per GPU rise from 22 to 48-64 (a >200% increase), with a Rubin-class rack reported at 9,000-11,000 inductors. `(30-50 -> 80-120 / GPU 22 -> 48-64)` [S4]
- Management states that rising GPU/ASIC shipment volumes and power (GPU power moving from 200-300W to 1kW+) directly drive higher inductor count, and that as Google/Amazon accelerate in-house ASIC deployment, 2026 is an important inflection point for demand. `(200-300W -> 1kW+)` [S2]

### Content & value growth
- Vertical power delivery (VPD) requires inductor thickness compressed from 8-9mm to 2-5mm, and the per-unit value of a TLVR inductor rises from ~$0.5 to $1-2.5 (a 3-5x uplift) — so content growth comes from both more parts and higher value per part. `($0.5 -> $1-2.5 (3-5x))` [S4]

### Market expansion
- Industrial Securities projects the broad AI chip-inductor TAM growing from roughly RMB2.5bn in 2025 to roughly RMB44bn by 2030 — a ~20x, five-year expansion, while domestic leaders still hold under 5% share today. `(RMB2.5bn (2025) -> RMB44bn (2030))` [S4]

### Physics necessity
- At kilowatt power and kilo-ampere transient currents, traditional ferrite wirewound inductors saturate and fail; high-end GPUs are abandoning them and alloy molded (integrated-formed) inductors with soft-saturation, low-loss, shielded characteristics have become a hard requirement rather than a BOM-cost choice. [S7]
- Infineon's first-of-its-kind TLVR four-phase module integrates four power stages, TLVR inductors and decoupling capacitors into a 9x10x5mm package delivering >2 A/mm^2 and up to 320A peak — confirming the power-delivery layer is consolidating into dense magnetic-plus-capacitor modules. `(>2 A/mm^2, 320A peak)` [S8]

### VPD leadership
- POCO is described as the outright leader in VPD chip inductors and supplies NVIDIA directly: the VPD chip inductor sits on the back of the GPU and a ~2mm-thick part withstands ~200A, without which voltage would swing beyond the chip's tolerance. `(2mm / 200A)` [S6]

### Vertical integration & margins
- POCO's 2025 gross margin was 42.06% versus an industry average of 23.88%, with a 23.0% debt-to-asset ratio below the industry's 34.04% — the profitability and balance-sheet edge of being integrated from powder to finished device. `(42.06% vs 23.88%)` [S5]

### Capacity ramp
- Chip-inductor shipments grew ~200% YoY in 2025, after the line's 2024 revenue rose ~275.76% to about RMB386m — the ramp is already in the numbers, not just guidance. `(+~200% (2025), +275.76% (2024))` [S3]
- POCO's new Huizhou high-end integrated-formed inductor line (phase 1) is set to come online in H1 2026; at full utilization chip-inductor capacity could reach ~250 million parts, roughly +400% versus current capacity, making POCO one of the clearest capacity-expansion plays in the segment. `(250M parts / +~400%)` [S4]

### Financial momentum
- 2026Q1 revenue was RMB490m (+27.73% YoY) with net profit of RMB88.52m (+20%), and 2025 operating cash flow reached RMB347m (+209.04%) — acceleration and cash quality are improving together. `(Q1 rev +27.73%, OCF +209%)` [S1]
- By 2026Q1 the inductor-component business accounted for over 30% of revenue, with mass-production breakthroughs in ASIC and optical-module applications. `(>30% of revenue)` [S2]

### Valuation ceiling

### Execution / ramp risk
- Sell-side notes explicitly flag the risk that new-capacity release and yield/delivery improvement could lag plan; 2025 H1 growth already slowed because end customers had not begun mass production — confirming the ramp is lumpy and timing-dependent. [S3]

### Competition & standardization
- 顺络电子 has highly overlapping high-current/thin-inductor products already shipping at scale into AI servers, and management notes the share of standardized chip-inductor products will gradually rise — both of which can compress the price/margin premium today's customized VPD parts enjoy. [S6]

### AI-capex cyclicality
- Analyst view (unsourced): chip-inductor content is a high-beta derivative of AI-server capex. The per-board content uplift is real, but if hyperscaler capex hits an air-pocket, a unit-volume cut can overwhelm the content story — this is a derivative of the AI buildout, not an independent secular driver.
