# Indium Phosphide (InP): The Second Bottleneck of AI Compute
*After HBM, the next physical chokepoint in AI scaling is the material that makes light*

> **As of** 2026-06-24  ·  **Generated by** AlphaThesis (an AlphaAnalyst extension)  ·  **Citation coverage** 100% (18/18 fact-claims sourced)

**One-liner.** Silicon does the compute and can route light, but cannot efficiently generate it. Indium phosphide does. No InP, no lasers, no hundred-thousand-GPU cluster — and the InP supply base was built for telecom, not for the AI demand curve it now faces.

## Verdict
Structurally bullish on the InP substrate / optical-interconnect bottleneck. The shortage is supply-side, certification-gated and concentrated, and it is anchored by hard hyperscaler capex commitments — not a narrative.

**Conviction:** High (supply-chain), with timing risk concentrated in CPO ramp and AI-capex durability.

## Bull thesis (constructive agents)
### Demand shock
- NVIDIA committed $4B in total ($2B each into Lumentum and Coherent) as joint R&D, purchase commitments and future capacity access, explicitly to tackle limited InP laser fabrication capacity for 800G/1.6T transceivers and CPO engines. `($4B)` [S1]
- AI capex is projected to reach $527B in 2026, pulling fiber, silicon photonics, 800G/1.6T and CPO from niche hardware into strategic AI infrastructure. `($527B)` [S3]

### Structural shortage
- Current transceiver demand is reported to exceed supply by roughly a factor of two due to InP constraints — a dynamic that mirrors the CoWoS advanced-packaging bottleneck of 2023-2024 that capped NVIDIA's H100 ramp. `(~2x)` [S2]
- McKinsey's networking-optics work expects 800G transceiver production to run ~40-60% below demand through 2027. `(40-60%)` [S3]
- 1.6T transceiver shortages are projected at ~30-40% into 2029. `(30-40%)` [S3]
- For 3/4-inch InP substrates, 2026 monthly demand is reaching ~700k-800k units while total global monthly supply is only ~400k. `(700-800k vs 400k / mo)` [S5]
- At OFC 2026 Lumentum's CEO said demand was running 25-30% ahead of supply, with the gap still growing. `(25-30%)` [S3]

### Concentration & pricing power
- A small group of ~3 suppliers (including AXT) together account for roughly 70% of global InP substrate output. `(~70%)` [S3]
- InP substrate is only ~2% of total optical-module cost, so downstream manufacturers are highly price-insensitive and focus on supply-guarantee agreements rather than price. `(~2% of module cost)` [S5]
- Suppliers such as AXT have implemented price hikes of nearly 70% for some customers; given InP's irreplaceable physics and high certification barriers, the shortage/price-hike wave looks highly sustainable. `(~70% price hike)` [S5]
- AXT disclosed a record ~$60M InP wafer backlog and is on track to double InP capacity by end-2026. `($60M backlog)` [S3]

### CPO amplifier
- CPO uses high-power CW light sources (300-400mW) with significantly larger die area than standard CW sources; as CPO ramps (expected to take off in 2027) it consumes InP substrate capacity at several times the per-unit rate. `(several x per unit)` [S5]

### Physics moat
- Unlike silicon or gallium arsenide, InP offers irreplaceable performance in high-frequency, high-speed, high-optical-power 1310nm/1550nm devices, making it the essential substrate for the copper-to-optical interconnect transition. [S5]
- Silicon can modulate and route light but cannot efficiently generate it; InP generates the laser light that carries data between GPUs, so there is no direct silicon substitute for the light-source function. [S3]

## Devil's Advocate (forced to disagree)
### Geopolitical / raw material
- China's concentration of global indium supply introduces a geopolitical risk that U.S.-based manufacturing investment alone cannot fully resolve without diversified upstream sourcing. [S2]

### Pricing-power ceiling
- Because InP is only ~2% of module cost, the same dynamic that grants supply leverage also caps how much absolute margin the substrate layer can ultimately extract before downstream pushes back or redesigns. [S5]

### Capacity catch-up
- AXT doubling InP capacity by end-2026 plus the broader 2-to-6-inch wafer transition means that if yields ramp as planned, the shortage thesis could soften faster than bulls expect. [S3]

### CPO timing
- The CPO demand multiplier is an expectation for 2027, not a realized volume; CPO has repeatedly slipped, so the largest single swing factor in the supply-demand model is also the least certain. [S5]

### AI-capex cyclicality
- Analyst view (unsourced): the entire optical-demand curve is downstream of AI capex. If hyperscaler capex disappoints or an AI-capex air-pocket hits, transceiver and InP demand can cut quickly — InP is a high-beta derivative of the AI buildout, not an independent secular story. [analyst view]

> ⚠️ **Unchallenged bull pillars (blind spots):** Physics moat — no opposing evidence in the knowledge base; treat with caution.
